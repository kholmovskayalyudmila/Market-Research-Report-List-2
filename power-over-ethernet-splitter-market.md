<p><p>A Power over Ethernet (PoE) splitter is a device that allows a network connection to be split into separate data and power streams. It takes the power and data from the PoE source and splits it into separate outputs for devices that require power over Ethernet but do not have built-in PoE capability. This allows non-PoE devices such as IP cameras, wireless access points, and VoIP phones to be powered by a PoE network.</p><p>The PoE splitter market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. Factors contributing to this growth include the increasing adoption of PoE technology in various industries, the rising trend of smart homes and smart buildings, and the advantages offered by PoE, such as cost-efficiency and ease of installation.</p><p>The market is also being driven by the growing demand for security and surveillance systems, as PoE splitters enable the installation of IP cameras in locations where power outlets are not easily accessible. Additionally, the rising deployment of wireless access points in corporate offices and the increasing adoption of voice and data communication systems are fueling the demand for PoE splitters.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in PoE technology, such as the introduction of higher power levels (PoE+) and the development of 60W and 90W PoE standards (Ultra PoE), are expected to drive the market growth. These advancements enable the powering of a wider range of devices and support higher power requirements.</p><p>In conclusion, the Power over Ethernet splitter market is predicted to grow at a CAGR of 4.40% during the forecast period. Passive splitters are simple devices without any additional circuitry, meaning they do not require any power source.
