<p><p>The Microscope Digital Camera market consists of two main types: CMOS and CCD. CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) cameras use an array of tiny light sensors to capture images, offering lower power consumption, faster readout speeds, and cost-effectiveness. CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) cameras use a silicon chip with light-sensitive diodes to transform light into electrical charges, providing higher image quality and sensitivity. Both types are widely used in scientific and research fields, with CMOS cameras being favored for their versatility and affordability, while CCD cameras excel in delivering superior image quality.</p></p>
