<p><ul><li>Double Layer</li><li>Pseudocapacitor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Organic supercapacitors are energy storage devices that utilize organic materials instead of traditional inorganic materials. They can be classified into two types: double layer and pseudocapacitors. Double layer supercapacitors store energy by separating positive and negative charges at the electrode-electrolyte interface, while pseudocapacitors store energy through reversible redox reactions at the electrode surface. Both types offer advantages such as high power density, fast charging/discharging, and long cycle life. However, characteristics like energy density and stability may vary between the two types.</p></p>
