<p><p>The broadcasting transmitter market consists of two types: analog and digital. Analog transmitters operate by modulating carrier signals with analog audio and video information. They have been historically used for broadcasting, but with the advancement of technology, digital transmitters have gained prominence. Digital transmitters convert audio and video information into a series of binary codes, known as bits, which can be easily transmitted and decoded by digital devices. Digital transmitters offer improved signal quality and bandwidth efficiency, enabling clearer reception and more channels to be broadcasted compared to analog transmitters.</p></p>
