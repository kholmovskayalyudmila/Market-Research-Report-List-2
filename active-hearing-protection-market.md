<p><p>Active hearing protection refers to a type of hearing protection device that actively detects and reduces harmful noise levels while allowing the wearer to hear and communicate effectively in less hazardous environments. This growth can be attributed to several factors, such as increasing awareness about the importance of hearing protection in high-noise working environments, stringent regulations regarding hearing protection in industries, and advancements in technology. <p><p>Active hearing protection refers to devices that help protect the ears from excessive noise while still allowing the user to hear important sounds. Earplugs are small, insertable devices that fit into the ear canal to block out noise. They provide a convenient and portable option for hearing protection. On the other hand, earmuffs are larger, over-ear devices that cover the entire ear to block out noise. They offer a higher level of protection and are often used in industrial settings or for prolonged exposure to loud noise. Both earplugs and earmuffs aim to reduce the risk of hearing damage caused by noise.</p></p>
