<p><p>The power optimizer market includes two main types: grid-connected solar microinverters and grid-off solar microinverters. Grid-connected solar microinverters are designed to convert direct current (DC) produced by solar panels into alternating current (AC) that matches the utility grid requirements. These microinverters enable each solar panel to work independently, optimizing power generation and minimizing shading or performance issues. On the other hand, grid-off solar microinverters are used in off-grid applications, where solar energy is stored in batteries and used when there is no access to the utility grid.</p></p>
