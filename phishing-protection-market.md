<p><p>The phishing protection market consists of two main types: email-based phishing and non-email-based phishing. Email-based phishing refers to attacks where cybercriminals send deceptive emails to trick users into sharing sensitive information or downloading malicious attachments. Phishing protection solutions for email-based attacks typically involve detecting and blocking fraudulent emails, educating users on identifying phishing attempts, and implementing email authentication protocols. On the other hand, non-email-based phishing encompasses attacks conducted through other communication channels like websites, social media platforms, text messages, or voice calls. Phishing protection for non-email-based attacks involves advanced threat detection and prevention mechanisms across various platforms and channels.</p></p>
