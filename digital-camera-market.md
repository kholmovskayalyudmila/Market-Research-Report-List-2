<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The digital camera market consists of two main types: built-in lens cameras, also known as point-and-shoot cameras, and interchangeable lens cameras. Point-and-shoot cameras are compact and have fixed lenses, making them user-friendly and suitable for casual photographers. On the other hand, interchangeable lens cameras include DSLR (digital single-lens reflex) and MILC (mirrorless interchangeable-lens cameras). These cameras offer more advanced features, higher image quality, and the ability to switch lenses, giving photographers greater creative control and flexibility. DSLRs use a mirror to reflect light into the viewfinder, while MILCs rely on electronic viewfinders.</p></p>
