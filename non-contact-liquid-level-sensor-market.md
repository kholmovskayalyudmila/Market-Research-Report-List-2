<p><ul><li>Ultrasound</li><li>Capacitive</li><li>Hydrostatic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The non-contact liquid level sensor market is segmented into three types: Ultrasound, Capacitive, and Hydrostatic. Ultrasound sensors use sound waves to measure the distance between the sensor and the liquid surface, offering accurate and reliable measurements. Capacitive sensors detect the change in capacitance caused by the presence or absence of liquid. They are suitable for detecting various liquid types and have high sensitivity. Hydrostatic sensors determine the liquid level by measuring the pressure exerted by the liquid. These sensors are commonly used in applications with corrosive or harsh liquids.</p></p>
