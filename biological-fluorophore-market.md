<p><p>The biological fluorophore market comprises various types such as Green Fluorescent Protein (GFP), Phycoerythrin, and Allophycocyanin. GFP is a widely used fluorophore due to its strong green fluorescence properties, making it suitable for a range of applications in molecular and cellular biology. Phycoerythrin is a red fluorescent pigment extracted from cyanobacteria and is commonly used as a marker in flow cytometry and fluorescence microscopy studies. Allophycocyanin, also derived from cyanobacteria, exhibits far-red fluorescence and is utilized for similar applications as Phycoerythrin. These fluorophores play vital roles in biological research, enabling visualization and tracking of specific targets within cells and tissues.</p></p>

<p><strong>What are the Emerging Trends in the Global Biological Fluorophore market?</strong></p>
<p><p>The global biological fluorophore market is witnessing several emerging trends.
